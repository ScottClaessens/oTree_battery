Welcome to the study how people interact and make economic decisions in groups. Please read the following consent form carefully.


Consent Form

You are invited to participate in this study because you are a participant in the New Zealand Attitudes and Values Survey, and you have agreed to participate in an additional online study. You must be at least 18 years old to participate.

The purpose of this project is to assess how economic decisions with others can predict attitudes and values. You will complete a series of interactions with other real participants. The decisions you make in these interactions will determine how much money you and they earn. The interactions are all slightly different, so you must read the instructions carefully and answer a comprehension question before each one.

The entire study will take approximately one hour. For participating in the study, you will earn $20. However, you can also earn a bonus payment on top of that amount ($25-30). The bonus payment you receive will depend on your decisions in the interactions. Your total expected payment from the study is $45-50.

There are no known risks associated with this research. Participation in this study is voluntary. You may withdraw from the study at any time.

Payment information will be asked from you at the end of this study. If your preferred method of payment is PayPal, we will ask you for your email address. If your preferred method of payment is a direct online bank transfer, we will ask you for your bank details. If your preferred method of payment is posted cheque, we will ask you for your full name and postal address. This information will only be used for payment purposes, and will be permanently deleted immediately after the payment is processed by the University of Auckland. After payment, nothing will directly connect you to your name or contact details. Your participant number will only be able to connect you to your previous NZAVS responses, which are anonymised.

The data from this study will be stored in an encrypted data file, which can only be accessed with a private key stored on a separate password-protected computer. The final anonymous data file may be shared with other competent professionals on a case-by-case basis for research purposes. It will not be possible for others to identify your responses from this data or subsequent reports.

If you have any questions or concerns about this study, please contact Dr Chris Sibley (Phone: 09 373 7599 ext. 87498; Email: nzavs@auckland.ac.nz) in the School of Psychology. You may also contact Dr William Hayward, Head of the School of Psychology, by phone (09 373 75 99 ext. 88516) or email (w.hayward@auckland.ac.nz).

For any queries regarding ethical concerns, you may contact the Chair, The University of Auckland Human Participants Ethics Committee, The University of Auckland, Office of the Vice Chancellor, Private Bag 92019, Auckland 1142. Telephone: 09 373 75 99 ext. 83711.
